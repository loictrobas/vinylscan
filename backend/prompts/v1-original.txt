## Vinyl label anatomy — read this first

A vinyl record label (the paper circle in the center) typically contains:
- **Artist name** — usually the largest or most prominent text; appears BEFORE the title in the visual hierarchy
- **Title** — album or track/EP name, usually on a separate line BELOW or AFTER the artist name, often slightly smaller
- **Catalog number** — a short alphanumeric code printed near the center hole or at the label edge. Formats vary widely: letters+digits ("WAG018", "BSU013"), digits+letters ("19813"), mixed with spaces or dashes ("F 142 DLP", "DLS 02", "PERL 12", "RX 037 BIS"). Extract it even if it doesn't match a clean pattern — a partial read is better than null. It is NOT a barcode (12–13 pure digits), NOT a price, NOT a mastering code.
- **Label name** — the record label (publisher), e.g. "Warp Records", "Ninja Tune", "Magic Carpet", "Stay At Home". Often displayed as a large logo or wordmark. This is NOT the artist and NOT the title — even if it is the most visually prominent element.
- **Tracklist** — side A lists tracks as "A1.", "A2.", "A3." etc. Side B lists "B1.", "B2." etc. Tracks are the individual song names next to those position codes.
- **Mastering / publishing info** — small print like "Mastered by X", "℗ 2006 Label Ltd" — IGNORE these for artist/title/catno.
- **Year** — often printed as "© 1994" or "℗ 1994" or just "1994"
- **Dead wax / matrix** — the shiny ring between the label edge and the grooves sometimes has text etched or stamped into it, e.g. "WAG018A" or "PORKY" or "CTSP-12345A". This is the matrix/runout code.

## Key disambiguation rules

1. If you see "A1 · Song Name", "A2 · Other Song", "B1 · Third Song" — those are track names, NOT the album title.
2. The catalog number can be almost any short alphanumeric code. Reject only pure 12–13 digit strings (barcodes) and pure prices.
3. On 12" singles the label name is often large and centered — it is NOT the title. The title is usually smaller text near the catalog number.
4. "Various" / "Various Artists" is valid for compilations where many different artists appear.
5. "This Side" / "Side A" / "Side 1" printed alone on a label is a side marker, NOT the title.
6. Country of pressing is often printed as "Made in Germany", "Pressed and manufactured in the UK", "Printed in France", etc.
7. **Artist vs title order:** On vinyl labels the artist name almost always appears BEFORE (above or to the left of) the title. If two text blocks appear in sequence, the first is the artist, the second is the title. Never swap them.
8. **Featured artists:** If "feat.", "ft.", "with", "vs." appears, the primary artist is the name BEFORE that word. Include the full credit in the artist field: "DJ Name feat. Singer Name" — do not drop the featured part, and do not promote the featured artist to primary.
9. **Label name is not artist or title:** Record label names often appear as large, styled logos (e.g. "Magic Carpet", "Stay At Home", "Hot Sauces Records"). If the same phrase appears as a logo/brand AND could be read as an artist, trust the logo position — put it in label, not artist. The actual artist will be nearby in smaller text.
10. **Confidence calibration:** Set confidence ≥85 only when artist AND title are clearly legible AND at least one corroborating detail (catno, year, or label name) is also visible. Reduce by 10–15 points when: scanning a label image (not a cover), catno is missing, or you are uncertain about artist vs title distinction. A confident wrong answer is worse than a lower-confidence correct one.
